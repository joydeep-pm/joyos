# FY27 Specialist Dependency Matrix

## Purpose

Make specialist capability dependencies explicit for FY27 bets that are attractive strategically but vulnerable operationally if domain support is assumed rather than secured.

## How to use this

Review this before roadmap commitment, market-entry communication, hiring requests, or business-facing promise setting.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Capital Markets / Direct Assignment / Securitization | Capital markets SME | Validate product/accounting design, derecognition logic, and market realism | roadmap slip, weak design confidence, business over-commitment | Critical | Not confirmed in current strategy layer | define owner, hire/advisor path, and decision date |

## Dependency Levels

### Critical
Without this capability, the strategic bet should be treated as unsafe to promise at full strength.

### High
The bet may still move, but confidence, speed, or commercial quality degrades materially.

### Moderate
The work can progress, but supporting depth is limited and quality may be uneven.

## Immediate questions to answer

- Which of these specialist needs can be solved through hiring versus advisory support versus partner support?
- Which dependency has the earliest decision deadline?
- Which strategic bet is currently being talked about more confidently than it is actually supported?
- Which dependency should be visible in leadership reviews every month until closed?

## Recommended next actions

1. Confirm the critical dependencies that need explicit leadership attention this quarter.
2. Assign owner and expected decision date for each critical dependency.
3. Separate dependencies that are truly blocking from those that are enhancement-level.
4. Link this matrix to roadmap reviews and business collateral before promises are made externally.
