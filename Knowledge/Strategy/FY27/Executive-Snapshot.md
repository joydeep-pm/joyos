# FY27 Executive Snapshot

## What this strategy is responding to

Finflux enters FY27 from a position of product strength in core LMS capability but with a serious commercial conversion problem.

The strongest repeated signal across the source corpus is not that the business lacks opportunity — it is that the platform repeatedly reaches evaluation and demo stages, then loses late because feature depth, compliance readiness, integration confidence, and category-specific parity are not strong enough to close.

## Headline facts

- FY26 confirmed India losses: **₹100.5 Cr** across 23 deals
- Demo loss rate: **78.5%** across 112 demos
- Gold parity: **~20%**
- LAMF / LAS parity: **~53%**
- BNPL / Credit Line parity: **~71%**
- FY27 roadmap scale: **~5,385 to 5,551 PD** depending on source version
- Team assumption: **~40–45 engineers + QA**

## Executive reading of the problem

This is primarily a **completion and credibility problem**, not an absence-of-roadmap problem.

The source materials repeatedly indicate that Finflux often gets invited into the room, but loses at the point where lenders expect:
- product completeness in high-value categories
- enterprise/compliance readiness
- confidence in implementation and integration quality
- stronger trust/referenceability against incumbents and specialized competitors

## FY27 strategy in one line

**Close the biggest parity gaps, remove compliance vetoes, open a few high-value new markets, and protect delivery quality hard enough that the business can convert pipeline into referenceable wins.**

## Core strategic moves

1. **Stop the revenue bleed in high-loss categories**
   - Gold
   - LAS / LAMF
   - BNPL / Credit Line
   - Vehicle / secured categories

2. **Pass the enterprise compliance firewall**
   - DPDPA
   - ECL / provisioning
   - EIR / accounting rigor
   - audit and governance improvements

3. **Open net-new markets deliberately**
   - Islamic finance / GCC / ASEAN
   - Capital markets / direct assignment / securitization
   - selected B2B / MCA / SCF adjacencies

4. **Recover delivery capacity and improve quality**
   - AI-assisted engineering leverage
   - bug-bash / bug-a-thon discipline
   - better implementation credibility and SI leverage

5. **Reduce non-product loss drivers**
   - trust deficit
   - incumbency advantage
   - poor referenceability
   - weak post-sale credibility and integration confidence

## Strategic conclusion

The FY27 plan is ambitious, but it is not random. The strongest justification in the source corpus is that many of the most painful FY26 losses map directly to identifiable EPICs, quartered investments, and capability gaps. The strategic challenge is therefore less about inventing a direction and more about sequencing, focus, and disciplined delivery.
