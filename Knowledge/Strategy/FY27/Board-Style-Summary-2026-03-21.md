# Board-Style Summary — 2026-03-21

## One-Line Read

FY27 strategy is directionally strong, but value realization now depends on disciplined protection of a few high-impact parity and compliance bets, stronger delivery credibility, and clearer ownership of specialist dependencies.

## What is going well

- The business has a clear view of where FY26 losses came from.
- The roadmap now has a more explicit recovery logic tied to known parity and compliance gaps.
- Strategy, follow-through tasks, and stakeholder-facing outputs now exist in one durable Personal OS system.

## What needs board-level attention

1. **Protected bets must stay protected**
   - Gold, LAS / LAMF, BNPL baseline parity, compliance gates, and stability/referenceability should not be diluted by adjacent complexity.

2. **Compliance is a growth enabler, not just a technical requirement**
   - DPDPA, ECL, EIR, and audit/governance depth influence whether enterprise opportunities remain viable.

3. **Commercial conversion still depends on trust and readiness**
   - Product progress alone will not close the gap if implementation confidence, proof assets, and references remain weak.

4. **New-market bets require explicit specialist support**
   - Capital markets, Islamic finance, and related expansion bets need visible capability ownership.

## Headline Facts

- FY26 confirmed India losses: ₹100.5 Cr
- Demo loss rate: 78.5%
- Major visible parity gaps: Gold, LAS / LAMF, BNPL / Credit Line
- FY27 roadmap scale: ~5,385–5,551 PD
- Team assumption: ~40–45 engineers + QA

## Current Risks

- scope drift or complexity expansion weakens protected bets
- compliance follow-through remains under-owned
- specialist dependencies may remain assumed rather than secured
- weak referenceability limits the commercial impact of roadmap progress

## Current Actions Underway

- quarter-protection review established
- win/loss review template established
- specialist dependency matrix established
- shareable business, roadmap, and executive output formats established

## Near-Term Focus

- confirm protected bets and deferrable work
- make win/loss analysis a recurring review rhythm
- assign ownership for specialist dependencies and business-facing proof needs
