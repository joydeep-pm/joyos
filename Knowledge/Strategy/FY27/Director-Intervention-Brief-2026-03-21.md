# Director Intervention Brief — 2026-03-21

## Strategic read

The current FY27 strategic risk is not lack of direction. It is lack of enforced follow-through.

The system now has:
- a durable FY27 strategy corpus
- a strategy-derived task layer
- a weekly strategy review workflow

What it does not yet have is enough live intervention against the highest-risk execution and commercial assumptions.

## Highest-leverage interventions

### 1. Protect the most commercially important FY27 bets

**Why it matters now**
The strategy corpus repeatedly shows that a small number of parity and compliance bets matter disproportionately. If these are diluted by adjacent scope or complexity, the FY27 recovery logic weakens quickly.

**Intervention required**
Force a quarter-protection review that separates:
- commercially mandatory bets
- strategically useful but deferrable work
- work that is expanding beyond its justified scope

**Primary operating goals supported**
- Stability
- New Business
- Documentation

### 2. Make win/loss learning an operating loop, not a retrospective artifact

**Why it matters now**
The FY26 loss patterns are one of the strongest assets in the strategy corpus, but they only matter if they keep influencing roadmap and communication decisions.

**Intervention required**
Create a standing review format and cadence for win/loss analysis, with explicit root-cause buckets and mapped implications.

**Primary operating goals supported**
- New Business
- Documentation

### 3. Force clarity on specialist dependencies behind new-market bets

**Why it matters now**
Capital markets, Islamic finance, and related strategic bets are attractive, but they remain vulnerable if specialist support is assumed rather than secured.

**Intervention required**
Turn specialist dependency risk into an explicit decision surface with role, advisor, partner, or hiring needs made visible.

**Primary operating goals supported**
- Team Leadership
- Stability
- New Business

## Strategic watchouts

- co-lending complexity can still crowd out clearer recovery bets
- compliance risk may still be under-owned relative to its commercial importance
- referenceability and collateral readiness remain easy to under-prioritize
- new-market narratives can outrun actual readiness if not forced through explicit dependency checks

## Today’s Three

1. Start the FY27 quarter-protection review note and define the 3-5 commercially mandatory bets.
2. Define the win/loss operating loop cadence, root-cause buckets, and expected output format.
3. List the specialist dependencies for capital markets, Islamic finance, and regional bets with ownership gaps.
