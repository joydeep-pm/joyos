# FY26 Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1: Getting shortlisted is not the same as being viable

The source materials suggest Finflux often got far enough to be evaluated seriously, but not far enough to be selected confidently.

### Meaning
The business has enough relevance to enter conversations, but not enough product depth and credibility in key categories to finish strongly.

## Lesson 2: A few category gaps cause disproportionate commercial damage

Gold, LAS / LAMF, BNPL / Credit Line, and vehicle-related capability gaps appear repeatedly in the loss and parity materials.

### Meaning
The business does not need equal investment everywhere. It needs concentrated investment in the few categories that repeatedly trigger loss.

## Lesson 3: Compliance is a veto layer, not a feature layer

Capabilities like DPDPA, ECL, EIR, auditability, and governance appear as enterprise gatekeepers.

### Meaning
A roadmap that treats these as secondary technical improvements will keep losing larger deals.

## Lesson 4: Trust and incumbency losses are real and expensive

Not all losses were caused by absent functionality. The source material explicitly highlights lack of trust, incumbent comfort, weak references, and implementation confidence.

### Meaning
Post-sale quality, references, and executive confidence are commercial assets, not merely delivery hygiene.

## Lesson 5: Integration and implementation credibility matter earlier than expected

Build-vs-buy and integration complexity show up in losses before detailed product parity even becomes the deciding factor.

### Meaning
Platform spine, config simplicity, implementation speed, and integration narrative all influence sales outcomes upstream.

## Lesson 6: Capacity concentration is itself a strategic risk

The plan places large pressure on a finite team while also requiring protected bug-bash and stabilization capacity.

### Meaning
The roadmap is not just about choosing the right work. It is also about preventing Q1/Q2 focus from collapsing under concentration and maintenance drag.

## Lesson 7: New-market bets require domain depth, not just product ambition

Islamic finance, capital markets, and international expansion show promise, but the sources repeatedly mention SME, certification, and design dependencies.

### Meaning
Entering these markets without domain support would likely recreate the same credibility problem in new categories.

## What FY27 must do differently

- protect the few parity-recovery bets that matter most
- treat compliance as a non-negotiable commercial gate
- protect quality and referenceability with explicit rituals
- make integration/configuration credibility part of the strategy story
- support new-market bets with real domain capability and governance
