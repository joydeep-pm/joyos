# FY27 Quarter Protection Review

## Purpose

Use this note to protect the most commercially important FY27 bets from dilution, scope drift, or avoidable execution slippage.

This is the first leadership review artifact that should be refreshed before roadmap reviews, business reviews, or stakeholder alignment meetings.

## Core question

Which FY27 items are commercially mandatory to protect, and which items are strategically useful but deferrable if they threaten execution quality?

## Commercially mandatory bets

These are the bets that appear most important to FY27 recovery logic based on the strategy corpus.

### 1. Gold parity and recovery stack
**Why protected**
- large visible loss base
- one of the clearest parity gaps
- directly tied to business credibility in a high-value category

**Protection risk**
- adjacent complexity or delayed recovery/legal depth weakens the commercial story

**Leadership ask**
- protect roadmap bandwidth and ensure end-to-end coverage is preserved

### 2. LAS / LAMF parity
**Why protected**
- repeated loss theme
- visible parity weakness
- strong recoverability logic if shipped well

**Protection risk**
- can be under-prioritized if broader ecosystem/platform scope expands

**Leadership ask**
- keep LAS/LAMF positioned as a recovery bet, not a nice-to-have extension

### 3. BNPL / Credit Line parity
**Why protected**
- recurring commercial gap area
- required to stay credible in modern retail / credit-line conversations

**Protection risk**
- loss of focus to adjacent roadmap items

**Leadership ask**
- force clarity on what “minimum commercial parity” means and protect that scope

### 4. Compliance firewall items
**Why protected**
- DPDPA, ECL, EIR, auditability, and related governance depth are deal-survival gates

**Protection risk**
- treated as secondary technical improvements rather than commercial veto removers

**Leadership ask**
- treat compliance items as protected business enablers, not backlog fillers

### 5. Stability / referenceability work
**Why protected**
- trust and implementation confidence materially affect conversion
- product parity wins will not convert if execution remains weak

**Protection risk**
- bug-bash / stabilization cycles are easiest to cut under pressure

**Leadership ask**
- ring-fence quality work as revenue protection

## Strategically useful but deferrable if needed

These are still important, but they should not quietly destroy certainty on the highest-confidence recovery bets.

- broader ecosystem expansion beyond justified Q1/Q2 scope
- optional polish work without clear commercial linkage
- any new-market storytelling that is outpacing actual readiness
- work whose only justification is strategic attractiveness rather than immediate revenue recovery or gating value

## Key quarter-protection questions

### Scope protection
- Which 3-5 items are truly non-negotiable this quarter?
- What work is currently expanding beyond its justified scope?
- Which initiatives are consuming leadership attention without clear recovery logic?

### Dependency protection
- Which protected bets depend on specialist capability, partner alignment, or external approvals?
- Which dependencies are still assumed rather than secured?

### Commercial logic
- Which items directly improve win probability this year?
- Which items remove enterprise vetoes?
- Which items improve trust/referenceability rather than just product depth?

### Slip consequence
- If each protected item slips by one quarter, what business consequence follows?
- Which slip would hurt revenue recovery most?
- Which slip would damage business confidence most?

## Recommended review output

At the end of the review, produce:
1. a list of protected bets
2. a list of deferrable items
3. explicit stakeholder asks / decisions
4. named risks that need follow-up before the next roadmap/business review
