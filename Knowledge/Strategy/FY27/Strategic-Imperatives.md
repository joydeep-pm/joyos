# FY27 Strategic Imperatives

## 1. Close the revenue bleed

The first imperative is to recover revenue that is being lost because Finflux is visibly behind in a few commercially decisive product categories.

Repeatedly referenced high-gap or high-loss areas:
- Gold Lending
- LAS / LAMF
- BNPL / Credit Line
- Vehicle / lease / fleet categories
- selected secured and mortgage-adjacent flows

### Why this matters
These are not abstract roadmap opportunities. They are directly tied to known FY26 losses and repeated competitive defeats.

### Operating implication
Q1 and early Q2 work must be protected from being diluted by lower-value scope or integration over-expansion.

## 2. Pass the compliance firewall

The second imperative is to remove the set of enterprise and regulated-lender vetoes that stop otherwise viable deals.

Key gates repeatedly cited:
- DPDPA compliance
- ECL / provisioning maturity
- EIR / revenue recognition rigor
- auditability and governance depth
- NPA / regulatory process depth

### Why this matters
These capabilities do not merely improve the product. They determine whether Finflux can remain in consideration for banks and large NBFCs.

### Operating implication
Compliance work must be treated as deal-gating infrastructure, not discretionary roadmap scope.

## 3. Enter selective high-value markets

The strategy does not only defend current categories. It also proposes entry into a few high-value markets where Finflux does not yet have strong revenue presence.

Primary bets:
- Islamic Finance for GCC / ASEAN
- Capital Markets / Direct Assignment / Securitization
- SCF / MCA / selected B2B adjacencies

### Why this matters
These bets create non-linear upside if executed well, but they also require domain expertise and sequencing discipline.

### Operating implication
These should be treated as focused market-entry programs with explicit capability, hiring, and certification dependencies.

## 4. Use AI to recover capacity, not to create theatre

The strategy frames AI as practical leverage for engineering and product differentiation, not as generic hype.

Expected roles:
- engineering productivity support
- testing/scaffolding efficiency
- visible product intelligence features
- later premium pricing leverage

### Why this matters
AI is one of the few levers that can expand throughput without linear headcount growth.

### Operating implication
AI should stay tied to measurable capacity recovery and product ACV uplift, not broad speculative experimentation.

## 5. Build a loss-prevention system, not just a feature roadmap

The source corpus explicitly notes that not all losses are caused by missing product features.

Material non-product drivers include:
- trust deficit
- incumbent comfort
- weak references
- poor implementation confidence
- integration complexity concerns

### Why this matters
If these issues are not addressed, even parity improvements may fail to convert into wins.

### Operating implication
FY27 needs operating mechanisms like:
- hypercare-to-reference loops
- structured QBR discipline
- win/loss closed loop reviews
- better product marketing and proof
- reference cluster building

## Bottom line

The strategic imperatives are strongest when read as one system:
- parity closes the obvious gaps
- compliance keeps deals alive
- market-entry bets create upside
- AI restores leverage
- loss-prevention improves conversion quality
