## Executive Summary

Finflux’s FY27 product strategy is now being run against a clearer operating model: protect a small number of high-impact parity and compliance bets, keep delivery quality visible, and ensure roadmap progress translates into commercial confidence. The strategy itself is clear; the current leadership priority is enforcing follow-through on the few bets that matter most.

## Current Status

### Overall status
- **In progress / needs continued leadership protection**

### What moved since the last update
- FY27 strategy has been consolidated into a durable operating layer instead of a browser-local artifact.
- The most important strategic priorities have been clarified:
  - high-value parity recovery
  - compliance gating work
  - stability/referenceability
  - specialist dependency management for new-market bets
- Supporting review and communication artifacts are now in place:
  - quarter-protection review
  - win/loss review template
  - specialist dependency matrix
  - business/shareable status templates

### What is currently in progress
- protection of the most commercially important FY27 roadmap bets
- creation of a recurring win/loss operating loop
- clarification of specialist dependencies behind capital markets / Islamic / regional bets

## Risks / Watchouts

- **Risk:** Scope or complexity expansion dilutes the highest-value recovery bets  
  - **Why it matters:** Revenue recovery depends on a small number of parity and compliance items landing well  
  - **Mitigation / next step:** Use quarter-protection review to separate protected bets from deferrable work

- **Risk:** Compliance items are treated as technical improvements instead of commercial gates  
  - **Why it matters:** DPDPA, ECL, EIR, and audit depth influence deal survival with enterprise stakeholders  
  - **Mitigation / next step:** Keep compliance work explicitly protected in roadmap reviews

- **Risk:** Product progress may not convert into business confidence  
  - **Why it matters:** Trust, references, and implementation confidence remain important non-product drivers  
  - **Mitigation / next step:** Improve collateral, proof, and referenceability alongside roadmap progress

- **Risk:** New-market bets outrun actual readiness  
  - **Why it matters:** Capital markets, Islamic finance, and related bets require specialist capability and disciplined sequencing  
  - **Mitigation / next step:** Use the specialist dependency matrix to drive explicit ownership and decisions

## Decisions / Support Needed
