# Weekly Strategy Review Workflow

A 20-30 minute weekly review to keep FY27 strategy, follow-through tasks, and next-week interventions current.

## Goal

Use the current strategy corpus, goals, and strategy-derived tasks to answer:
- what strategic work moved
- what strategic risk is drifting
- what commercial or delivery assumptions need re-checking
- what follow-through is stale
- what the next week’s must-win interventions should be

## When to Do It

- Friday afternoon after the main week has settled
- Sunday evening before planning the next week
- Monday morning before meetings start crowding out strategy thinking

## Use These Inputs

- `GOALS.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/Operating-Command-Brief.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/AOP-vs-Roadmap.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/Win-Loss-Analysis.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/LOS-Strategy.md` (when relevant)
- strategy-derived task files under `Tasks/`

## Review Sequence

### Step 1: What moved in strategy follow-through?

Prompt:

```text
What moved this week across FY27 strategy follow-through?
```

Group the answer by:
- roadmap protection
- win/loss learning
- specialist dependency follow-through
- collateral / referenceability
- AI-native operating improvements

Look for:
- decisions made
- reviews completed
- stakeholder alignment created
- follow-ups closed
- strategy notes updated

### Step 2: What strategic risk is drifting?

Prompt:

```text
What strategic risks, assumptions, or roadmap bets are drifting right now?
```

Focus on:
- parity bets losing protected bandwidth
- compliance work slipping
- co-lending or adjacent complexity expanding too far
- new-market bets outpacing readiness
- quality or referenceability being under-protected

For each, note whether the right move is:
- escalate
- clarify
- de-scope
- protect
- follow up

### Step 3: Which strategy-derived tasks are stale?

Prompt:

```text
Which strategy-derived tasks are stale, blocked, or not translating into action?
```

Review at least:
- `Tasks/fy27-strategy-review-and-quarter-protection.md`
- `Tasks/fy26-win-loss-operating-loop.md`
- `Tasks/fy27-domain-hiring-and-specialist-dependency-plan.md`
- `Tasks/fy27-collateral-and-referenceability-improvement-plan.md`

Look for:
- tasks with no recent progress
- tasks that need owner clarity
- tasks that should become actual meetings, artifacts, or decisions next week

### Step 4: What changed in the business read?

Prompt:

```text
What changed in my reading of the business this week?
```

Look for changes in:
- confidence in key roadmap bets
- commercial readiness
- stakeholder alignment
- dependency risk
- urgency of specialist or collateral work

If something changed materially, update the relevant FY27 strategy note.

### Step 5: What should the system learn?

Prompt:

```text
What should Personal OS learn from this week's strategy follow-through?
```

Capture:
- recurring strategic blind spots
- decisions that should become durable notes
- templates or review structures that are missing
- repeated blockers that should become standing watch items

Prefer to update `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/` or `Knowledge/Learnings/` rather than leaving the learning in memory.

### Step 6: Plan next week’s must-win interventions

Prompt:

```text
Help me plan next week's must-win strategic interventions.
```

Return:
- no more than 3 priority interventions
- the one decision or meeting each intervention needs
- the one artifact or follow-up needed to support each intervention

## Example Output

### Strategic Progress

- Protected one roadmap discussion from scope drift
- Clarified one specialist dependency for a new-market bet
- Updated one strategy note with a sharper business read

### Drifting Risks

- Co-lending complexity is expanding relative to parity recovery work
- Compliance follow-through is under-owned
- Referenceability remains weaker than roadmap ambition suggests

### Stale Follow-Through

- Win/loss operating loop task has not yet been turned into a recurring format
- Collateral improvement task needs a real owner and timeline

### Next Week’s Must-Win Interventions

1. Run the quarter-protection review and force explicit scope protection decisions
2. Turn win/loss analysis into a reusable review template and cadence
3. Clarify domain-specialist dependencies for capital markets / Islamic / regional bets

## Quick Version (5 minutes)

If time is tight:

```text
Quick weekly strategy review: What moved, what drifted, what follow-through is stale, and what are next week's 3 must-win strategic interventions?
```

## Tips

- Keep this review separate from the generic weekly review; this one is for strategy refresh and intervention quality.
- Do not turn it into a long backlog session.
- If a strategy note changes materially, update the note the same day.
- End with 1-3 interventions, not a long list of chores.
