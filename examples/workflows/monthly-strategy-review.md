# Monthly Strategy Review Workflow

A 30-45 minute monthly review to assess bigger strategy movement, communication readiness, and the next month’s most important interventions.

## Goal

Use the current strategy corpus, stakeholder-facing outputs, and strategy-derived tasks to answer:
- what materially changed this month
- where strategy confidence improved or weakened
- what business-facing message should change
- what risks are becoming structural rather than temporary
- what the next month’s must-win interventions and communication outputs should be

## When to Do It

- last working day of the month
- first weekend of the next month
- before monthly business or roadmap review meetings

## Use These Inputs

- `GOALS.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/Operating-Command-Brief.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/Current-Shareable-Status.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/Business-Status-Update-*.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/FY27-Quarter-Protection-Review.md`
- `Knowledge/Strategy/FY27/FY27-Specialist-Dependency-Matrix.md`
- `Tasks/fy27-*.md`

## Review Sequence

### Step 1: What materially changed this month?

Prompt:

```text
What materially changed this month in FY27 strategy, business confidence, and roadmap follow-through?
```

Look for:
- shifts in confidence on key bets
- decisions made or delayed
- risks that grew or reduced
- changes in stakeholder alignment
- any business-facing message that should now be different

### Step 2: What moved from “watch” to “decision”?

Prompt:

```text
Which strategy items now require explicit leadership decisions rather than passive watching?
```

Focus on:
- protected bets at risk of dilution
- compliance items slipping into ambiguity
- specialist dependencies still unresolved
- trust/referenceability gaps affecting business conversations

### Step 3: What is the current business-facing message?

Prompt:

```text
If I had to brief stakeholders this month, what is the clearest current message?
```

Use this step to update:
- `Current-Shareable-Status.md`
- the next dated business status update
- any roadmap-facing summary if needed

### Step 4: Which risks are becoming structural?

Prompt:

```text
Which risks are no longer short-term noise and are becoming structural problems?
```

Examples:
- repeated scope expansion
- repeated under-protection of compliance
- recurring implementation-confidence weakness
- unresolved specialist dependency gaps
- strategy drifting away from actual delivery confidence

### Step 5: What should change next month?

Prompt:

```text
What are next month's must-win strategic interventions, decisions, and communication outputs?
```

Return:
- no more than 3 interventions
- no more than 3 communication outputs
- the one biggest risk that needs persistent tracking next month

## Example Output

### Material Changes
- One protected bet gained clearer executive support
- One specialist dependency remained unresolved and is now a decision item
- Business-facing status messaging needs to emphasize protection and readiness, not just roadmap ambition

### Structural Risks
- Co-lending complexity is staying too large relative to recovery confidence
- Referenceability remains under-owned

### Next Month’s Must-Win Interventions
1. Secure explicit protected-bet decisions
2. Resolve specialist ownership for new-market bets
3. Refresh stakeholder-facing status based on current confidence and risk

## Quick Version (10 minutes)

If time is tight:

```text
Quick monthly strategy review: What materially changed, what now needs a decision, what is the stakeholder message, and what are next month’s must-win interventions?
```

## Tips

- Keep this review broader than the weekly strategy review.
- Focus on changes in confidence, message, and structural risk.
- Use it to refresh stakeholder-facing artifacts, not just internal notes.
- End with the next month’s top interventions and communication outputs.
