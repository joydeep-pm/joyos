# 1:1 Prep Workflow

Prepare a focused 1:1 with a PM or key counterpart using tasks, meeting notes, people notes, and current blockers.

## Goal

Help Joydeep enter each 1:1 with:
- the right support questions
- the most important blockers
- coaching themes grounded in evidence
- clear follow-through after the conversation

## When to Use

Use this workflow before:
- monthly PM 1:1s
- support conversations with direct reports
- follow-up conversations after missed expectations
- developmental check-ins or feedback sessions

## Prompt Examples

```text
Prep my 1:1 with [PM name].
```

```text
What should I discuss with [PM name] in our next 1:1?
```

```text
Summarize blockers, wins, and coaching themes for [PM name].
```

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Gather evidence

Review:
- relevant tasks owned by or associated with the PM
- `Knowledge/People/[pm-name].md`
- recent meeting notes
- recent feature-request notes
- blocked or stale items involving the PM

### Step 2: Build the 1:1 brief

Summarize in this order:

#### 1. Wins / progress
- what moved since the last 1:1
- where the PM created momentum

#### 2. Current blockers
- what they are waiting on
- what is stuck because of unclear ownership, engineering dependency, or scope quality

#### 3. Coaching themes
Look for patterns such as:
- stories not ready for grooming
- weak follow-through
- inconsistent stakeholder communication
- good ownership and execution worth reinforcing

#### 4. Support Joydeep should offer
Recommend specific support actions:
- escalation help
- decision clarification
- stakeholder alignment
- artifact review
- prioritization guidance

### Step 3: Draft the conversation outline

Produce a short structure:
1. check-in / context
2. wins to acknowledge
3. blocker discussion
4. coaching theme
5. support or next actions

### Step 4: Capture follow-through

After the 1:1, convert important outcomes into:
- task updates
- people note updates
- coaching evidence or IDP-style notes
- follow-up reminders

## Example Output

### Wins
- Closed the LAS sizing loop faster than expected
- Improved stakeholder communication on the client clarification thread

### Blockers
- BNPL story set still lacks enough clarity for grooming
- Engineering dependency has been stale for 5 days

### Coaching Themes
- Strong ownership once scope is clear
- Needs to raise ambiguity earlier instead of carrying it too long

### Support Joydeep should offer
- Help escalate the engineering dependency today
- Review the user-story framing before the next grooming session

### Suggested 1:1 Outline
1. Appreciate progress on LAS thread
2. Discuss BNPL grooming-readiness gap
3. Explore what made the ambiguity linger
4. Agree on an earlier escalation pattern next time
5. Confirm support and next actions

## Tips

- Base coaching on examples, not vague impressions.
- Reinforce wins as clearly as blockers.
- If the same theme repeats across two or more 1:1s, capture it in the person note and bring it into weekly review.
- Keep the output practical enough to use immediately before the meeting.
