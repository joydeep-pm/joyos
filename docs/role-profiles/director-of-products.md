# Director of Products Role Profile

This profile defines the operating model the Personal OS should optimize for.

## User

- **Name:** Joydeep Sarkar
- **Role:** Director of Products
- **Company:** M2P Fintech
- **Domain:** Lending infrastructure
- **Core product scope:** Core Lending Suite
- **Primary charters:** Co-Lending, LAS, BNPL

## Role Mission

Help Joydeep decide where to intervene each day across product execution, team leadership, client escalations, documentation quality, and leadership communication.

This is not a generic productivity system. It is a local-first operating system for product leadership.

## Primary Operating Goals

### 1. Documentation
Keep PRDs, user stories, status updates, and product artifacts clear, complete, and current.

### 2. Stability
Surface bugs, engineering blockers, implementation gaps, and delivery risk before they become leadership or client escalations.

### 3. New Business
Support client-driven requests, roadmap inputs, sales / RFP asks, and strategic product opportunities.

### 4. Team Leadership
Support PM growth, 1:1 preparation, feedback capture, and coaching follow-through.

## Recurring Inputs

The system should expect these as common inputs into Joydeep’s week:
- client escalations
- PM-raised feature asks
- sales / RFP requests
- implementation gaps
- bug or stability issues
- engineering blockers
- leadership update requests
- grooming-session prep inputs
- meeting notes and transcripts

## Recurring Outputs

The system should help generate or prepare:
- PRDs
- user stories
- internal status updates
- monthly leadership product updates
- roadmap / AOP inputs
- prioritization decisions
- follow-up notes after escalations or meetings
- PM coaching and 1:1 prep notes

## Decision Lenses

When prioritizing or recommending interventions, prefer this order:
1. business or client risk
2. blocker age / staleness
3. leadership urgency or due date
4. impact on Documentation, Stability, or New Business
5. dependency impact on PMs or engineering
6. effort only after urgency and impact are clear

## Daily Operating Rhythm

### Morning
Produce a **Director Intervention Brief**:
- what needs intervention now
- who owns it
- why it matters
- what next action is required

Then produce **Today’s Three**:
- no more than three high-leverage items
- fewer on heavy meeting days

### During the day
Use the workspace to:
- capture raw items in `BACKLOG.md`
- update feature-request notes, people notes, and meeting notes
- convert follow-ups into tasks
- keep blockers and decisions visible

### Weekly
Run a structured weekly review that answers:
- what moved Documentation, Stability, and New Business
- what remained blocked
- which PM or client patterns repeated
- what the system should learn or improve

## Knowledge Model

The assistant should encourage these durable note types:
- `Knowledge/Feature-Requests/`
- `Knowledge/Meetings/`
- `Knowledge/People/`
- `Knowledge/Leadership-Updates/`
- `Knowledge/Grooming/`
- `Knowledge/Learnings/`

## Success Criteria For The OS

The Personal OS is working well when it helps Joydeep:
- quickly see where intervention is needed
- avoid overcommitting beyond Today’s Three
- keep leadership artifacts moving without last-minute scrambling
- reduce forgotten follow-ups from meetings and escalations
- spot recurring blocker patterns across PMs, engineering, and clients
- capture learnings that improve the system over time
